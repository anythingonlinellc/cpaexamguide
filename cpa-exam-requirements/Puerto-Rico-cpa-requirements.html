Puerto Rico Requirements Overview:

Puerto Rico's (PR) CPA exam requirements include that you must be a US citizen and that you be at least 21.

Follow each caption below to ensure that you are qualified to sit for the exam.

Education Requirements

What are the education requirements in Puerto Rico?

150 Hours Requirement

PR requires 150 hours or graduate degree to sit and become a licensed CPA

NOTE: You must maintain a minimum GPA of 2.0

Accounting Course Requirements

PR requires 38 semester hours in accountingÂ 

Business Course Requirements

PR requires 32 semester hours excluding accounting hours

Business Law Requirements

PR does not have a specific Business Law requirement

Ethics Exam

Required to take AICPA Ethics Exam


Age & Residency Requirements

What are the age and residency requirements in Puerto Rico?

US Citizenship Requirement

You are NOT Required for Puerto Rico to be US Citizen to sit for the CPA exam

Residency Requirement

Puerto Rico does NOT require you to be a resident, employee, OR have an office in the state for you to take the CPA exam.

Minimum Age to Sit for the CPA Exam

Puerto Rico does not have a minimum age requirement to sit for the CPA exam

Puerto Rico CPA Exam Fees

How much does it cost to take the CPA exam in Puerto Rico?

Puerto Rico has fairly standard exam fee rates. If you need to re-take a part there is a $50.00 re-application fee that you will have to pay.
