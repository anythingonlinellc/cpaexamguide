QBank And The SmartPath™



Roger CPA Review prides itself on the work its data scientists create for their students.

Within their study platform they provide you with what's called the QBank which contains two types of study tools.

SmartPath Predictive Technology™

Roger CPA Review prides itself on the work its data scientists create for their students.

The SmartPath is their latest creation!

Because Roger CPA Review tracks the results of all of their students, they know which students pass and which don’t.

They are then able to look at the questions that the students who pass have completed and gotten correct and then compare that to your results.

Based on your data they are then able to predict how ready you are to sit for the CPA exam.

Now as a CPA exam mentor this makes me very excited to have this kind of data, but in my opinion comes with a bit of a caveat.

In order to pass the CPA Exam, you MUST understand the material.

Don’t assume just because you got a question right that you understand WHY the question is right or wrong.

Understand what the SmartPath is.

A tool to show you where your strengths and weaknesses are.

Once you know your weaknesses, then you know WHERE to spend your time studying.

This is why implementing the U.C.E.G. Method is crucial to your success.

It helps you tailor your time, what you study and what you review on a daily basis all while using the SmartPath as your guide.

Targeted Practice Tests

As you complete the learning phase of studying with Roger CPA Review you will want to keep track of everything you have learned up to that point.

Creating a quick practice test within the Roger CPA Review platform allows you to tailor different topics to test yourself on.

You also have a "tutor mode" which instantly reveals the answer which is great for teaching yourself as you take the test.

There is also a timed mode which helps you with time management and you will have an opportunity to check your results after you complete the test.

You can also test yourself only on MCQs or Sims.

Segment your test to only show you unused questions, questions you've gotten incorrect, flagged questions or just all questions available.



Question Analytics


One of the areas that have been lacking within Roger CPA Review in the past was their multiple choice analytics.

What I look for in a CPA review course is that they give you the ability to look at the sub-topic level of each topic to determine where you need to spend your time studying based on your performance.

So how does the new 2019 version of Roger CPA Review perform?

Well the good news is that they have improved significantly by adding the SmartPath technology to their CPA exam question test bank.

You can now determine how prepared you are (and what your confidence level is) at the topic level.

However, the overall analytics still aren't at the sub-topic level.

For example within AUD 1 there are 17 sub-topics, but when I look at the multiple choice analytics data, I only see my overall results for all 17 sub-topics.

This makes it difficult to know which sub-topics I am struggling with and which topics I'm dominating at a high level.

The good news is that EACH question you answer ties to the sub-topics and links directly to it.

This makes up for the lack of high level analytics but does add an extra step to the process.

Another feature that the Roger team said is on the way, will be a way to tie the analytics from the course outline to the AICPA blueprints.

This is an important feature to have because of how NASBA gives you your score release results if you fail.

You need to be able to tie where you scored weaker to the Roger CPA Review analytics so you can determine what you need to study.

If you download the AICPA blueprints and compare it to the course outline it's not hard to do but a bit of a manual process since Roger teaches the blueprints out of the order they are listed.



Task-Based Simulations


Roger CPA Review does their best to prepare you with in-depth simulations, but you need to understand that the AICPA has only released a few real simulations that you will see on the exam.

So while Roger CPA Review has great simulations and makes great guesses to what you will see, it's important to understand that no one other than the CPA exam creators know what the simulations on the exam will be like.

Best advice is to do your best to understand the material so that it doesn't matter what simulation you get on exam day.

That's why learning from the Roger video lectures and textbook are crucial to your success and not the number of questions you take.

But compared to Roger CPA Review's competition, Roger CPA Review's simulations get the job done for prepping you for the real thing.



Brand New Flashcard Functionality


My favorite feature within the new Roger CPA Review platform is the ability to create your own flashcards within the platform itself.

As you are taking a practice test and there is a topic you want to remember, they make it simple to create your front and back of the flashcard within the app itself.

Very valuable for when you want to quickly review topics you know are difficult to understand.

As always, Roger CPA Review also includes their own digital flashcards that are organized by the course outline and can be flagged and marked as learned.

You are also able to access all your flashcards on any device you own that can load Roger CPA Review.

Roger CPA Review Frequently Asked Questions


Q: How Long Do I Get Access To Roger CPA Review?

A: Roger CPA Review has multiple options for you in terms of software access. Their most expensive Elite Unlimited packages includes unlimited access, their Premier Package gives you 18 months access and if you buy a single section, you will receive 12 months access.

Roger CPA Review Versus Becker

A: Roger CPA Review is fairly similar to Becker CPA Review in terms of the content included. HOWEVER the way the material is presented is very different.

Becker CPA Review focuses more on the LONG lectures to teach you the material. You can compare Becker to Roger CPA Review side by side here.

Roger CPA Review in my opinion has much more engaging lectures and equals the quality of multiple choice questions offered by Becker CPA Review.

At $3,393 for all four sections, Becker CPA Review is significantly more expensive that Roger's CPA prep course. With course packages starting at $1,695, Roger CPA Review is right in the middle in terms of industry average for pricing for CPA exam courses. You also need to consider course access when investing in a CPA review course. Becker's software expires after 18 months while Roger's Premier gives you 24 months and their Elite Course package gives you unlimited access until you pass.

In my opinion, if you need video lectures to learn, I would hands down go with Roger CPA Review over Becker CPA Review.

Roger CPA Review Versus Wiley CPAexcel

A: Roger CPA Review in many ways is very similar to Wiley CPAexcel but has some key differences. You can compare Wiley CPAexcel to Roger CPA Review side by side here.

Roger CPA Review focuses much more on video lectures in their primary course and cram course than Wiley CPAexcel.

Roger CPA Review also focuses on having the right CPA exam questions instead of massive quantities of questions like Wiley CPAexcel. This is great until you fail a section and then you will be forced to find outside test banks so you can utilize fresh CPA exam questions to study.

Wiley CPAexcel also offers unlimited access to their software until you pass for all three of their course offerings, Roger CPA Review only provides this for their Elite Course Package customers.

The biggest difference between the two courses lies in their multiple choice analytics. As we discussed above, Roger CPA Review is shifting to use their "SmartPath" technology which identifies which topics you need to focus on based on other students data. This is a great way to track your progress, but if you want more control over what you study it becomes a bit more difficult. Wiley CPAexcel tracks your data all the way down to the sub-topic, Roger CPA Review does not currently do this.

Who Should Use Roger CPA Review?

A: Roger CPA Review is absolutely for anyone who is an auditory learner who needs a classroom setting to learn. I would also recommend Roger CPA for anyone with a short attention span who needs an instructor who will (in an entertaining way) make the complex understandable.

Roger CPA Review has plenty of multiple choice questions and simulations to get you across the finish line and the SmartPath technology is a move in the right direction for the multiple choice analytics offered by Roger CPA Review.
