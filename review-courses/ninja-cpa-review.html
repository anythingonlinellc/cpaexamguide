Wondering if Ninja CPA Review is legit?

Well you are in luck!

I've known Jeff for quite some time and have had several opportunities to chat with him via email and phone and I'm going to breakdown his CPA review course for you below.

IMPORTANT NOTE: Jeff and I do not have an affiliate relationship nor does he pay any advertising on this site. So that you don't have to give away your email address to get your free trial, I purchased a months subscription to Ninja CPA Review on my own so that you can understand what you get before buying and then compare what I see with the other big name courses that are out there.

Now with that being said....

Here's my take on the course after signing up for the Ninja CPA Review subscription.


Who is Ninja CPA Review?

Ninja CPA Review is masterminded by Jeff Elliott, CPA of Another71.com.

His pay by the month CPA review course includes his Ninja CPA study book + study notes + audio + a multiple choice and simulation test bank that was developed in house + some very old video lectures leftover from Bisk CPA Review which Jeff purchased.

 

What I Like About Ninja CPA Review

The course includes a full textbook that is based on the AICPA blueprints, also includes notes that you can use to quickly review topics, includes audio of the notes + audio covering some multiple choice and BLITZ videos done by Jeff himself and digital flashcards.

The highlight of the program is absolutely the brand new test bank that Jeff and his team put together.

It includes both multiple choice and simulations + the ability to create practice or mock exams.

Has in-depth multiple choice analytics to help you identify your strengths.

Just be aware that these questions are from the released questions by the AICPA that the majority of CPA review courses use. So if you already have a CPA review course there will be overlaps with the same questions and answers.

It's also hard to beat the price.

$67/month is very reasonable for the amount of resources that you receive.

Jeff has also done amazing things for CPA candidates for many years by providing a free forum for us to commiserate and help each other.

 

What I Don't Like (And It's Super Weird)

An additional feature, that I'm not sure why Jeff includes, is what's called the NINJA PLUS videos from the very old Bisk CPA Review.

The video lectures are very outdated and mostly presented by the now deceased Bob Monette of Bisk CPA Review.

Jeff gives a very vague disclaimer on each video:

"NINJA PLUS is the Bisk CPA Review Video Course that we acquired from Thomson Reuters in 2016. This course previously cost $2,000. We also acquired the rights to the Bisk Hot Spots, which cost an additional $5,000 retail at one point (each Hot Spot video was around $130 each). You'll notice that they are a little dated (we will post updates with each video as we encounter outdated content), but people who have used them - love them - especially the videos with Bob Monette, who is universally regarded as one of the top CPA Review instructors ever. Sure - some of the videos are boring, and the instructor may put you to sleep, but they are a gold mine of concepts that could give you a distinct edge on Exam Day."

 

Then he puts the effort on you to notify him if you see something that is outdated... come on Jeff!

 

"Content Updates: In an effort to keep this amazing video series current, please email support@ninjacpareview.com with anything you see that needs updating content-wise. If possible, please tell us which video and where it's located in the video. Thank you for your contributions to the NINJA Community and keeping these CPA Review Materials effective and affordable for everyone!"

 

Personally, if I was using Ninja CPA Review, I would avoid the Ninja Plus videos at all cost because you don't have time to waste questioning if something is up to date or not.

For this reason I can't recommend Ninja CPA Review as a primary study resource.

Self-studiers, who learn great from reading the text, NEED an instructor from time to time to explain tough topics to him/her.

As the AICPA says, you need study resources that will help you understand the blueprints as quickly as possible and in a way that will help you master the higher learning skills required now to pass the CPA exam.

And outdated video lectures aren't a great way to do that.

Plus watching videos of Dr. Monette, who passed away several years ago, has a real Weekend At Bernie feeling...

 

 

Final Verdict:

Now I will say that after diving into Jeff's review course that I did find the software fairly easy to use.

All the questions come from the AICPA released questions and the unofficial answer key.

This means that they are the same questions that basically every CPA review course uses to populate their test banks.

He provides you with multiple choice analytics which make it easy to track how you are doing at a content group level.

He also includes simulations for you to answer which is great.

The NINJA notes and audio are also great ways to save time and give yourself the ability to quickly review everything you need to know for exam day.

You get access to a private study group which is nice, but it's basically just an anonymous forum with a smaller number of people than his free forum.

So my verdict is that I would recommend Ninja CPA Review for anyone who needs additional help supplementing their primary CPA review course and needs study notes

OR

If you are someone who doesn't need video lectures to learn the material, then you may be able to get away with using Ninja CPA Review as your primary course, but I would not advise it if you are serious about passing.

But I would try the big name CPA review courses such as Wiley CPAexcel, Roger CPA Review, Yaeger CPA Review or Gleim CPA Review first before making that decision.

Ninja CPA Review does offer a free trial of their test bank, but you have to wait several days once you are their email list to try it for 3 days. I also confirmed that they have a 30 day money back guarantee which I did find is very easy to contact support to request for one.
