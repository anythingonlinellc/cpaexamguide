Becker CPA Review Pros & Cons

Is Becker CPA Review Worth It?

✓ Video Lectures + Textbooks + Mobile App

Written materials are top notch with plenty of examples and mnemonics and the lectures are fairly engaging but are essentially just a page by page walkthrough of the text. Several lecturers have a lot of personality and some can be quite corny, but they will keep you focused. The accounting lectures do run a bit longer than most other CPA review courses. Everything can also be accessed and used on their mobile app for complete freedom as you study.

✓ Multiple Choice & Task Based Simulations

Becker CPA Prep Materials includes 9000+ CPA exam questions and answer explanations. Each questions includes a detailed explnation that you can tie back to the source text but I did find quite a few examples where the explanations were a bit thin compared to other courses I have reviewed. The highlight for me though was their SkillMaster Videos where they teach you step by step how to answer various Task Based Simulations.

✓ Unlimited Access

After years of limiting software access, Becker CPA review FINALLY provides accounting students and CPA candidates unlimited access until they pass. This is important because if life gets in the way of the exam, you don't want to scramble to find a new course in the middle of studying. NOTE: This only applies to the Premium and Pro packages. Advantage only offers 24 months access.

✓ Success Coach & Tutoring

Depending on the Becker package you choose you may have access to a CPA exam success coach and/or 5 hours of 1-on-1 tutoring sessions. This isn't quite at the same level as a CPA exam mentor, but it certainly helps to have additional help.

✖ Locked Down Platform

From a usability perspective, one of the most frustrating things about Becker is that you can't select any of the text inside of the platform. Other courses allow you to select almost any text and then create a flashcard in 5 seconds from that text. I assume they do this to curb piracy, but it really hurts your ability to grab important pieces of text from the question explanations or text and drop them into a word doc or a flashcard maker for later review. They also have a note taking system for each module inside the lecture but then don't give you a way to create a database of notes to search thru later. The only reason to take digital notes is to be able to keyword search them later to save time but you can't easily do that with Becker.

✖ Does Not Bundle With Ultimate CPA Exam Guide

Becker CPA Review does not currently bundle with Ultimate CPA Exam Guide which means if you need additional mentorship or tutoring you will have to purchase each plaform separately.

✖ Standard Customer Service

According to their website, they have very limited hours on the weekend. Expect 2 full business days for a response per their website. The majority of students study during the weekend and need a quick response which in my opinion shouldn't be more than 24 hours. When sending in an email I would recommend you batch your questions together to speed up the process.

Which CPA Review Course Has The Highest Pass Rate?

Every CPA review course claims they have the highest pass rate and for obvious financial reasons.

For example, Becker claims that a whopping 94% of their students pass the CPA exam. Similarly, Wiley CPAexcel and UWorld Roger CPA claim 90-91% of their students pass.

Sounds great right! Well...

As a former auditor for PwC, I don’t rely on advertised pass rates, marketing buzzwords or fancy technology to make my recommendations and neither should you.

Always look at the fine print anytime a CPA review course publishes their pass rates and you will notice that they rely on students to self-report and it’s not independently calculated or verified.

What I did was blindly trust a CPA review course who promised a 90%+ pass rate which gave me a false sense of security.
